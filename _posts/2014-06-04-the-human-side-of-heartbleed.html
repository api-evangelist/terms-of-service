The Human Side of Heartbleed The announcement on April 7 was alarming.A new Internet vulnerability called Heartbleed could allow hackers to steal your logins and passwords.It affected a piece of security software that is used on half a million websites worldwide.Fixing it would be hard: It would strain our security infrastructure and the patience of users everywhere.It was a software insecurity, but the problem was entirely human.
