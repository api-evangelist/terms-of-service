Twitter is investigating after 55,000 account details — including username and password combinations — were published online.Account details seemingly belonging to spammers were uploaded to Pastebin, a code-sharing site often used by hackers to post the results of their hacking escapades.The accounts were published over five Pastebin pages — one, two, three, four, five.Legitimate users who are on the list are advised to change their passwords immediately.A Twitter spokesperson said the company was looking into the situation.
