Just a couple of weeks ago, MOZ.com, the popular internet marketing software advised all its customers to reset their MOZ account passwords, because the encrypted portion of some of the member passwords were made public for a brief time.About a month back, online daily deal company LivingSocial Inc.alerted its 50 million users to reset their account passwords following a cyber-attack on their computer systems that resulted in unauthorized access to some customer data from their servers.On March 2, 2013, Evernote revealed that hackers had gained access to their network and been able to access user information, including usernames, email addresses, and hashed passwords.
