Continuous delivery makes it possible to keep web apps updated and fresh.Increasingly, continuous delivery is also making it possible for manufacturers of hard goods to update their products, too.Eric Minick’s article in DZone highlights the “internet of things” and how online updates of embedded systems in consumer products are enabling a world in which products actually get better after you buy them.The cell phone in your pocket, the thermostat on your living room wall, the dashboard of your car — all of these are as updatable as web apps.Their manufacturers upgrade them in exactly the same way as web apps and other software: by periodically pushing out new code to fix bugs or add new features.
