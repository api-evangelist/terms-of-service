Evernote, after suffering a data breach that caused the company to reset passwords for all of its 50 million users, announced that it plans to adopt two-factor authentication as quickly as possible.We were already planning to roll out optional two-factor authentication to all of our users later this year, said Evernote spokeswoman Ronda Scott via email.We are accelerating those plans now.More Security Insights Webcasts Serving Two Masters: The Secret to Successful Identity and Access Management Deployment Understanding What Data Masking Approach is Right For You Evernote warned all users Sunday via email that the company had suffered a security breach, after the companys operations and security team discovered and blocked suspicious activity on the Evernote network that appears to have been a coordinated attempt to access secure areas of the Evernote Service.Attackers appeared to have accessed a list of Evernote users usernames, email addresses and passwords, which the company described as being protected by one-way encryption, saying they were both salted and hashed.
